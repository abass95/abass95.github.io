Mortgage Pre-Approval

When you’re looking to purchase a property a mortgage pre-approval is often a smart idea. A pre- approval will allow you to be pre-approved by a lender for up to a certain amount of money before you even start looking for properties. This way you know exactly how much money you qualify for. A pre- approval will lock in your rate for 120 days so it allows you to take advantage of good rates without the risk of rates going up. If rates go up during the 120 days, your mortgage would remain at the lower locked-in rate. If rates drop within that time frame, then lenders will generally honor the lower rate. So it’s really a win-win!

Refinance

Refinancing your mortgage means you are getting a new mortgage to replace your existing one. Why would someone want to do this? Well one reason is if someone wants to capitalize on lower interest rates thus decreasing the interest rate they are paying on their loan. A second reason is that someone wants to take out equity (cash) from their home for another purchase, renovations, or other opportunities. Another reason is that someone wants to consolidate other debts (credit card, line of credit, car payment) into one debt (your mortgage) so that they are paying less interest, or lowering their overall monthly payment. Whatever the reason may be, refinancing your mortgage can be a great option for many homeowners. Unsure if a refinance can help you? Feel free to contact me so that I can help you determine if a refinance makes sense for you.

Switch/Transfer

A switch mortgage or transfer mortgage involves moving your current mortgage from one lender to another without changing anything except the term and interest rate. A switch is beneficial when you want to take advantage of lower rates in the market without changing any other aspect of your mortgage. Within the term, there will be a penalty for breaking the mortgage, though often the savings in moving to another lender with a better rate will substantially outweigh the penalty. Doing a switch at the end of your mortgage term will allow you to completely avoid the penalty. Unsure of how much you can save, what your penalty will be, and if it’s all worth it? Feel free to contact me and I will break everything down for you.




Special Programs and Products



Home Equity Line of Credit

A Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC) is a revolving line of credit that enables you to borrow from the equity in your home. As you pay off your mortgage and as your property appreciates you are able to take this money out at an interest rate that is much lower than traditional lines of credit. You are only charged interest on your HELOC for the portion that you use, similar to a traditional line of credit. Therefore if you take out a $200,000 HELOC, but are only using $50,000, you are only charged interest on the $50,000.
